Welcome

This page contains links to some of the projects I worked on over the last few years. The main goal of my PhD research was to improve success rates and clinical outcomes after in vitro fertilization (IVF) through better prediction of embryo viability.

Toward this goal, I noninvasively gathered optical and biomechanical data about embryos at various stages of development and built a model to accurately identify those with high viability. I then used this model to understand what goes wrong at the molecular level when an embryo fails to develop successfully.

Projects

The projects are listed below. You can also view the code for each project on my GitHub profile @liviaz.

  1. Predicting egg and embryo viability based on measurements of their optical and biomechanical properties

  2. Identifying patterns of gene expression associated with high developmental potential

  3. Assessing freezing-induced damage on embryos based on automated image analysis

  4. Modeling embryo development as a partially observable Markov decision process (POMDP)

  5. Automated measurement of embryo biomechanical properties
